401(k) Plan To be eligible to join the 401(k) Plan, an employee must complete 3 months of service and be 21 years of age or older. The employee may join the Plan on the first day of the 3 months of the calendar year.
Health Insurance Your health and well-being are a priority of the USC. Information on enrollment will be made available during your new hire orientation. You should submit your enrollment request within the time frame established during your orientation to elect this coverage.
Dental Insurance and Vision Care You are eligible to participate in USC’s dental and vision care program if you are a full-time employee of USC.
Consolidated Omnibus Budget Reconciliation Act (COBRA) If you are covered under one of USC’s health plans, you have the right to choose to continue coverage for yourself, your spouse, and/or your covered dependents for a prescribed time period, should you lose your coverage due to a reduction in work hours, a Personal Leave Without Pay, military leave of absence, or termination of your employment. In case of such an event, you will be notified of the termination of your coverage and your COBRA rights and responsibilities. At such times, you must elect this COBRA coverage in order to continue insurance coverage under the USC group plan. If you choose to continue group benefits under COBRA, you will be responsible to pay the total applicable premium, plus Cobra administrative fee. Coverage will cease if you fail to make premium payments as scheduled, become covered by another group plan that does not exclude pre-existing conditions, or become eligible for Medicare. For detailed information or questions on COBRA, please contact Human Resources. Maternity Leave USC offers a total of 8 weeks of unpaid maternity leave. If a new mother needs additional time off, she may be eligible to apply for State Disability Benefits according to state policy. USC is firmly committed to protecting the rights of expectant mothers and complying with Title VII of the 1964 Civil Rights Act as amended by the Pregnancy Discrimination Act of 1978. USC’s policy is to treat women affected by pregnancy, childbirth, or related medical conditions in the same manner as other employees unable to work because of their physical condition in all employment aspects, including recruitment, hiring, training, promotion, and benefits. Workers’ Compensation This plan provides a maximum weekly amount set by the management team if your illness or injury is job-related. Depending on the nature of the disability, Workers’ Compensation insurance may provide income replacement in addition to payment for medical expenses. Employees on leave associated with Worker’s Compensation will retain continuity of service. Benefits accrued at the time the leave began will be available to the employee upon return from leave, but no additional benefits, including vacation or sick time, will accrue during the leave.
